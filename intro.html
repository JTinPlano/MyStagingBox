Grand Opening

Welcome to MyStagingBox.com, the perfect staging area for ALL things hot rod. If it's a hot rod happening, you'll find it here. See "Event Types and Frequency" below for more information.

Registration

Why should you register with MyStagingBox.com? It's quick, easy, and free. Being a registered member gives you complete control of your profile, gives you access to the live event schedule, allows you to list your event, and all of the other features of the site.

Event Listing

Submit all of the information only one time and it's available for up to 5 years. Only the owner of the event will be able to access the listing to edit, postpone, or cancel it. Take a Test Drive

Event Schedule

The event schedule covers the entire US and is sorted by date first, then state, then city, then start time. This makes it very easy to browse the event listings to locate a particular event or an event in a specific city or state. Take a Test Drive

Advertising

To advertise your business, product, or service, you have to be registered. Ads that will be accepted include graphics (jpg, gif, png, or flash), text, or a combinatiom of both. I will provide the specs for each type of ad that will be available, including a small ad in the right column, a banner ad to be displayed on the event schedule, and an "Event of the Month" to be displayed at the top of the schedule. The "Event of the Month" will be restricted to one per event per year.

Only the owner of the ad will be able to access the ad to edit, postpone, or cancel it. This section of the site is currently in development and will be available soon, although I can't predict when that will be. If you've registered and checked the "Newsletter" box, you will be notified when it's live.

Help at Your Fingertips

You'll find help scattered all over the site to answer questions you might have. Just mouseover the symbol anywhere you see it and a description/explanation box will open and close automatically.

About Me

MyStagingBox.com has been, and will continue to be, a labor of love. I am a child of the 60's and remember muscle cars in dealer showrooms, and having friends who got them as graduation gifts. My first almost-muscle car was a 1968 LeMans, 350, 4-bbl, 4-speed with AC, PS, and PB. That's still my favorite car to this day, although I've had two restoration businesses that ultimately failed and took me down the tubes financially. Fast forward to the late 1980's.

I moved to Dallas to go to a computer maintenance and repair school and found that I had a knack for troubleshooting and fixing computers. Then I took a couple of C and C++ programming classes and found that I REALLY liked programming. Fast forward to the mid-1990's.

I was introduced to the Internet in 1996 and logged in to AOL in November of that year. As a result, I taught myself how to build web pages, then added PERL scripting, went to work for a dot com, learned Java and database SQL.

I finally settled for PHP and MySQL as my web development tools of choice and have been playing around with that type of Internet technology. In 2004, I met a couple who ran car shows and found I liked them. I also learned that it was a pain finding a web site with a list of car shows and that it was an even bigger pain to get a car show scheduled on any of the sites of which I knew. More recently, I've learned the latest "Web 2.0" technologies upon which Facebook, Twitter, and many other web sites are built. Those technologies are the basis for this, my first, Web 2.0 site.

I just wanted to let everyone know why I built this site: My love of the classic and muscle cars, and web site development. This has been a 3-year labor of love, and all of your donations will be greatly appreciated.
